      After years of using the laser printer method with ExpressPCB and no way to have those boards professionally made, I finally figured out how to convert expresspcb files to gerber without paying. This only works for 2-layer boards at the moment, but I'm working on a way to do 4-layer.

      What you need:
      ExpressPCB
      Adobe Acrobat Professional
      LinkCAD http://www.linkcad.com/

      What you probably want:
      VMWare

      Heres how to do it.

      1. Print each layer of your board to a separate PDF using the Acrobat Pro printer. Do not use a different PDF printer, it probably won't work.

      2. Open each PDF in Acrobat and save as a PostScript file.

      3. Open LinkCAD, select PostScript to Gerber

      4. Enter these settings:
      First Page:
      Page number: 1
      Arc resolution: 32 segments/360
      UNCHECK Treat white areas as holes
      Check Ignore clipping paths
      Second Page:
      Leave everything default except select Use circular interpolation

      5. After the file is converted, go to Tools, Convert Quasi-circles to circles
      Precision: 300DPI
      Minimum vertex count: 8 (works for smallest via in ExpressPCB)

      6. On your top layer, rename Filename_white.gbr to something.drl, this is your drill file. On other layers, delete the _white.gbr file

      If you want to be able to keep doing this after 5 days, install everything in VMWare, turn off guest clock sync, and take a snapshop right after the installation. Every time the trial expires, just restore the snapshot.

                I just wanted to re-iterate some of what was said. I use expresspcb to do propfessonal PCB's that my company turns into products. We have frequently used ExpressPCB's service to obtain the Gerber files when we want to build pcb's in large qty's. The fee is about $60 and you usually get the files emailed to you within 24 to 48 hours. You simply have to email their support group and ask for the gerbers, giving them the order number that was provided you when you paid for the pcb's. These files contain all of the information necessary for any board house to produce your boards, including the necessary information for automated bpard stuffing and soldering equipment.


                -Bill
